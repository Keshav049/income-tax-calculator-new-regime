## What Is the Income Tax Calculator New Regime?

The new tax regime was introduced under **Section 115BAC** of the Income Tax Act. Starting FY 2023-24, it became the default regime for all individual taxpayers — meaning if you didn't actively choose the old regime, the new one applied automatically.

For FY 2025-26, Budget 2025 revised the slabs again. The basic exemption limit went up to ₹4 lakh, the Section 87A rebate was increased to ₹60,000, and the standard deduction for salaried individuals now stands at ₹75,000. Together, these changes make income up to ₹12.75 lakh effectively tax-free for salaried taxpayers.

The trade-off is that you give up most popular deductions — 80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest — in exchange for these lower rates. Whether that trade works in your favour depends entirely on your income and deduction profile.

This **income tax new regime calculator** applies all the latest FY 2025-26 slabs, rebates, and cess rules as notified by the Income Tax Department. You get the accurate final number, not an approximation.

## New Tax Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26)

These are the revised slabs under Section 115BAC for Assessment Year 2026-27, updated after Budget 2025:

| Annual Income (₹) | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 20% |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | 30% |

> **Section 87A Rebate:** The rebate covers up to ₹60,000 in tax for resident individuals with net taxable income up to ₹12,00,000 — making their final tax liability zero. For salaried individuals, the ₹75,000 standard deduction pushes this effective tax-free threshold to ₹12,75,000. A 4% health and education cess applies on all tax computed above this limit.

For the official slab structure and rebate rules, the Income Tax Department's [FAQ page on new vs old tax regime](https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/new-tax-vs-old-tax-regime-faqs) is the most reliable source to cross-check your figures.

Yes. The **online tax calculator new regime** supports non-salaried income too. For details on how the new regime applies to business and professional income, the IT Department's [guidance page for salaried and non-salaried individuals](https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/individual/return-applicable-1) has the complete breakdown.

**Q5. Is the new regime really the default for FY 2025-26? What if I want the old regime?**
Yes, the new regime under Section 115BAC is the default. If you want to file under the old regime instead, you need to actively opt for it while submitting your ITR. Salaried individuals can do this directly in the ITR form — no separate declaration needed. Taxpayers with business or professional income need to file Form 10-IEA. The [official page on the default regime and opt-out process](https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/individual/return-applicable-2) explains this clearly for different taxpayer categories.
