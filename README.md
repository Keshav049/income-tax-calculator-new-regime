## What Is the Income Tax Calculator New Regime?

The new tax regime, introduced under **Section 115BAC** of the Income Tax Act, was made the default regime for individual taxpayers starting FY 2023-24. For FY 2025-26, the government revised the slabs further to make it more attractive — especially for salaried individuals with fewer deductions.

Under this regime, taxpayers forgo most common deductions (such as 80C, 80D, and HRA) in exchange for lower slab rates. The standard deduction of ₹75,000 is, however, available under the new regime for salaried employees.

## New Tax Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26)

The following slabs apply for individual taxpayers opting for the new regime under Section 115BAC for Assessment Year 2026-27:

| Annual Income (₹)       | Tax Rate |
|--------------------------|----------|
| Up to ₹3,00,000          | Nil      |
| ₹3,00,001 – ₹7,00,000   | 5%       |
| ₹7,00,001 – ₹10,00,000  | 10%      |
| ₹10,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 15%      |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000 | 20%      |
| Above ₹15,00,000         | 30%      |

> **Note:** A rebate under Section 87A is available for individuals with net taxable income up to ₹12 lakh, effectively making tax liability nil in that bracket. A 4% health and education cess applies on the total tax amount.

The answer depends entirely on your deduction profile.
